Government communication on customer decision-making and consumer sentiment in periods of panic buying

This report investigates into herd behaviour during times of scarcity, particularly in response to government communication amid crises like the COVID-19 pandemic. By examining three surveys with different government messaging approaches, it suggests that effective government communication eases consumer fear, potentially reducing panic buying tendencies. Findings from 76 participants in 2022 highlight the significant impact of government communication on consumer behaviour, underlining the need for strategic communication and policy recommendations to address future instances of panic buying

SPSS (Statistics), Qualtrics, NVIVO
